GitHub User Information Awareness Survey

Survey Introduction

Dear Participant(s),

Thank you for being willing to participate in our study for the awareness of user information on GitHub.

We are a research group from Vanderbilt University, led by our principal investigator Dr. Yu Huang (yu.huang@vanderbilt.edu). Responses to this survey will help us to evaluate the severity of unintended exposure of personal information on GitHub and create effective means to protect user information in the future. This survey should take around 5 to 10 minutes to complete, and no personally identifiable information will be collected or linked to the results of this survey. Study results will only be analyzed in aggregates.

In this study, we will ask you about public commits that are associated with you by email or name but are also associated with other suspicious identities. We will also ask a few general questions and some demographic questions at the end of this study. Upon completion, you may enter an email address for a chance to get a $25 Amazon eGift Card through a ruffle. The ruffle for reward will be random, and we guarantee that there will be at least one winner for every 50 participants. All rewards will be sent out through email within 5 business days after this survey closes.

If you have any questions during or after the study, please contact Anda Liang via emails: anda.liang@vanderbilt.edu. You are free to withdraw from this study at anytime you want. Before we begin the study, a consent form will be prompted, with more detailed explanation about this study.

Thank you again for your participation!
